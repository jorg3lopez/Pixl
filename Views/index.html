PIXL: The Planetary Instrument for X-ray Lithochemistry on Mars 2020

PIXL is a micro-focus X-ray fluorescence instrument for examining fine scale chemical variations in rocks and soils on planetary surfaces.

PIXL can measure elemental chemistry of tiny features observed in rocks, such as individual sand grains, veinlets, cements, concentrations and crystals using a 100 µm-diameter, high-flux X-ray beam that can be scanned across target surfaces. Making close-up measurements of the elemental chemistry of rocks is a fundamental capability for surface-based explorations of rocky planets.
